What the name means.

A numeronym counts what's hidden between the edges. T, fifteen letters, N. Thibaut Tiberghien. The same construction as a11y — accessibility — and i18n — internationalization. The name does something specific: it asks you to do a small amount of work.

The reader who figures it out has participated in the naming. That's different from a name that just announces itself. And there's something right about that for a blog that's going to be about the work of thinking — the effort of figuring out what you actually believe, written down where it can be read and tested.

The territory this covers: knowledge infrastructure. The cognitive debt that accumulates when teams use AI without a substrate to hold the thinking. What it actually means to work in an agent-native way inside a real company, not a hypothetical one. Why opinionated tools win. What design-led companies do differently. The Mesh — not as a pitch but as a worked example of a problem and how it got solved.

This is the pipeline test. The Worker assembles the chrome, the post renders, the RSS feeds, the theme persists. Once that's confirmed, this gets replaced with actual writing.
